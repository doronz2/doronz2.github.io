Privacy Policy — The Social Evidence

Last updated: July 6, 2026

The Social Evidence is a browser extension and Android app for collaborative fact-checking, operated by Doron Zarchy as a non-commercial project. This policy describes what data the apps handle and how.

What we collect

When you post an annotation or a reply, the following is sent to and stored on our server:

  1. The text you highlighted, a small amount of surrounding page text (used to re-locate the highlight), and the address (URL) of the page it appears on
  2. The comment text you wrote
  3. The display name you chose (any name is accepted; it does not have to identify you)

This data is public by design: annotations are shown to every user who visits the same page. When you only read annotations (without posting), the app sends the current page's URL to the server to look up annotations for that page; these lookups are not stored as a browsing history.

What we do not collect

  1. No accounts, emails, passwords, or phone numbers
  2. No location data, contacts, or device identifiers
  3. No advertising, analytics, or tracking of any kind
  4. No sale or sharing of data with third parties for their own purposes

Automated processing

When a new annotation is posted, its text may be compared against previously posted annotations from the same website using an AI service (Anthropic's Claude API) to detect contradictions between public statements. Only annotation text already stored on our server is sent for this comparison.

Where data lives

Data is stored in MongoDB Atlas and served through Google App Engine (United States). Your display name is kept on your own device (browser storage or app preferences) so you don't have to re-enter it.

Reporting and removal

Any user can report a comment as abusive or spam from within the app; comments reported by multiple users are hidden automatically. To request removal of content you posted (or content about you), email doronz@gmail.com with a link to the page — removal requests are honored.

Children

The apps are not directed at children under 13.

Changes

Changes to this policy will be posted at this address. Continued use of the apps after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
